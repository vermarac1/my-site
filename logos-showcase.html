Usage guidance

Logo 01 — Use when

The mark + wordmark combination works best in the website nav, email signatures, presentations, and anywhere a recognisable visual symbol is useful alongside the name. The ink circle with the oxblood diamond creates a compact, strong favicon and app-icon candidate.

Logo 02 — Use when

The typographic logo works best in editorial contexts: PDF report headers, proposal cover pages, LinkedIn banners, and anywhere the serif typeface reinforces the advisory-firm positioning. The hairline rules above and below give it a masthead quality. The oxblood S distinguishes it from a plain wordmark.

Colour rules

On light backgrounds (paper, white): ink mark, ink text, muted sub-label.

On dark backgrounds: swap ink → #FAF6EC. Oxblood accent stays unchanged on both. Never use the dark-bg logo on a light surface.
