# Meera Nambiar — Target Customer Review

**Who I am:** Co-founder and CEO of a B2B SaaS company in India. $8M ARR, 3-year-old product, Series A closed 14 months ago. Pipeline has stalled. I hired an agency 9 months ago. Fired them 6 months ago. I am now looking for something different, and I am not sure what.

**Assignment:** Am I convinced? What questions do I still have?

---

## Verdict: You got my attention. You did not get my trust.

---

### How I Got Here

I found this site through LinkedIn. Someone in my network shared a post. I clicked through with low expectations — I get pitched by 3 "revenue architects" a month. I read the hero headline. I stayed.

---

### What Convinced Me (Partially)

The hero headline spoke directly to my frustration. I have been saying to my co-founder for months that "we're doing everything right and it's still not working." Seeing that reflected back — "not failing because of effort, failing because of the system" — made me feel understood.

The three structural failure modes (Positioning gap, ICP too broad, Qualification misalignment) are accurate to my situation. I recognise these. They have been described to me before by people who then sold me a retainer to "fix" them over 12 months. The difference here — fixed scope, 5–7 days for the Audit — is interesting enough to keep reading.

The case files are reassuring in structure. A $2M pipeline result in 9 months from zero is something I understand. The NaaS B2C-to-B2B case is less relevant to me but shows range.

---

### Questions I Still Have (And Could Not Find Answers To)

**1. Why should I trust that these results are real?**

All three case studies are anonymous. "Names withheld under engagement terms." I understand why, but this is the first time I'm encountering this brand. There is no external signal — no press mention, no LinkedIn article, no mutual connection who can verify. One named client with permission, or a verifiable third-party mention, would change this entirely.

**2. What does an engagement actually look like week by week?**

The Audit is described as "5–7 business days." What happens on day 1? What do I need to provide? Who do I talk to? The Pipeline Sprint is "4–6 weeks" but there is no process description. I am about to trust someone with my company's most sensitive commercial data — pipeline numbers, ICP definitions, CRM access. I need to know what I am buying, not just what I will receive at the end.

**3. The two operators: what is the division of labour?**

Rachit's background is clear and directly relevant. Priyanka's bio mentions "Gartner-aligned frameworks" and "end-to-end digital transformations." Those are very large corporate descriptors. I am an 8M ARR company. I do not want a Gartner framework. I want someone who has been in my situation. Her bio does not tell me when or where she has done that.

**4. If I book the Diagnostic Call, what happens next?**

There is a Calendly link. I click it. I book. Then what? Is it a discovery call or a working session? Will Rachit be on it? What should I prepare? The current CTA gives me no information about what I'm walking into.

**5. This company launched in April 2026.**

The site does not say this, but I can tell from context signals. "SCALEBUZ launched April 2026. Independent client engagements signed: 0." I know because I looked carefully and there are no dates on the case studies that post-date early 2025. The case studies are from in-house roles. That is disclosed. But the gap between "I did this at previous jobs" and "I will do this for you as an independent consultancy" is real. What has changed? Why now? The methodology section hints at this but never makes the leap explicit.

**6. Why is there no indication of cost anywhere?**

Not even a directional range. Not even "Engagements are structured as fixed-scope, not hourly." I understand the strategy — qualify first, price second. But I have been on three agency calls in the past year where I spent 30 minutes explaining my situation and then got a proposal for 4x my budget. One sentence acknowledging that this is a premium, fixed-scope service with a "let's see if there is a fit before we discuss investment" framing would remove my hesitation about wasting 30 minutes.

---

### Would I Book the Call?

Honestly: 50/50.

The hero worked. The case files worked. But there are too many unresolved trust questions for a first-time visitor with no prior relationship. If I had seen a LinkedIn post from Rachit first — something that demonstrated his thinking in his own voice — the conversion rate would be much higher. This site is not designed to convert cold traffic. It is designed to convert warm traffic that arrives already partially convinced.

For cold traffic, the conversion gap is the Diagnostic Call description. Tell me exactly what happens in those 30 minutes.
