# Panel Discussion — ScaleBuz Website Review

**Present:** Shreya Iyer (Brand), Marcus Tan (Design), Ankit Verma (Growth), Meera Nambiar (Target Customer), Rohit Kapoor (Investor)

---

## Opening Round: What Is The Single Biggest Problem?

**Rohit:** I'll start where I always start: the numbers. The $200M+ pipeline stat and the $2M case study number on the same page is a credibility bomb. I don't care how you explain it — "pipeline influenced vs revenue attributed" is a distinction that requires education to understand. Every analytical buyer will flag it. Fix it before anything else.

**Ankit:** Agreed on the stat — that's my top conversion risk too. But I'd argue the CTA exhaustion is just as damaging for different reasons. The page has 9 "Book a diagnostic" buttons. You're training visitors to ignore the button because it appears before they're ready. First CTA hits, second hits, by the third it's visual noise. You've turned your primary conversion mechanism into wallpaper.

**Marcus:** My biggest problem is the logo. I know that sounds like a designer's obsession, but hear me out. The site has one of the best editorial design systems I've seen in B2B consulting. Bone paper, oxblood, Newsreader italic — distinctive, premium, confident. And then you look at the brand mark and it's a CSS circle with a dot. It looks like the placeholder before the actual logo gets designed. The entire premium design system is undermined by the thing that appears first on every page.

**Shreya:** The logo bothers me too but I want to name something that goes deeper. This site has no human voice. There are 774 lines of HTML and not a single line sounds like it was written by a person who has been frustrated, who has failed, who has learned something the hard way. "The diagnostic structure behind every ScaleBuz engagement" — that is a robot summarizing a thing a person built. The case studies have outcomes but no narrative. The team bios are formal. You've written a document about a person without the person being present. For a business where the product *is the person*, that is a structural problem.

**Meera:** I want to agree with everything said but add the one thing I couldn't get past as the actual buyer: I don't know what I'm walking into when I book the call. Thirty minutes. One specific observation. Fine — but what should I prepare? Will Rachit be on the call? Is this a pitch or a working session? The Diagnostic Call is the entire conversion mechanism and it has no description. I'm being asked to book something I don't understand. Some of us won't click unless we know what we're clicking into.

---

## Point of Disagreement: How Bad Is the "Zero Clients" Problem?

**Rohit:** It's a real problem but it's also not fixable with copy. Every startup service business has a zero-client moment. The question is whether the site handles it honestly or evasively. Right now it's evasive — it presents in-house case studies as if they're consulting case studies. A better approach: own the launch, make the in-house background the explicit foundation, and use the Diagnostic Call as the risk-reversal mechanism.

**Meera:** I'd push back slightly — it's not evasive, it's just incomplete. The methodology section says "built through in-house accountability" which is close. But it doesn't say "this is a new practice built on 15 years of in-house results." Making that explicit would convert more cold traffic.

**Shreya:** The distinction is whether you're performing experience or describing it. The current copy performs it. "Our methodology" — who is "our"? You're two people who launched two months ago. The site reads like a McKinsey practice with institutional depth. The actual story — one operator who has built this eight times and is now doing it independently — is more compelling than the institutional performance.

---

## Agreement: The Services Grid Is a Credibility Leak

**Ankit:** Eight service tiles from two operators. Strategy, Brand, Demand, Authority, Lifecycle, AI, Web, Events. I don't want to seem like someone who can't do 8 things. But a B2B SaaS CEO looking at this page will think: "If they're selling everything, they're probably not excellent at anything."

**Marcus:** The design issue is related — the grid looks like a capability menu from a 50-person agency. Two operators cannot be personally accountable for 8 discrete service lines at premium rates while also doing the diagnostic and build work on live engagements.

**Rohit:** From an investor perspective: it signals strategic confusion. You've positioned as a diagnostics-first, architecture-build firm. Then you have a services section that looks like a rate card. Which is it?

**Meera:** The services I actually care about — ICP definition, positioning, pipeline systems — are buried in a grid with AI automation and event strategy. I almost missed the parts most relevant to me.

---

## Agreement: The Nav Label "Operators" Has To Go

**Ankit:** First-time visitor: "Operators — is that like their ops team? Their software operators? Their consulting methodology?" The word has too many meanings. "Team" communicates instantly. "Operators" requires context that new visitors don't have.

**Marcus:** Also the word shows up in the section header too — "Operators · 08". Keep the internal brand language internal. Navigation has to be immediately parseable.

**Meera:** I almost skipped that section because I assumed it was about their internal operations. I'm glad I read it because the bio sections are strong, but the label nearly lost me.

---

## Priorities — Where the Panel Agrees

**All five agreed as P0:**
1. Remove or justify the $200M+ pipeline stat (stat discrepancy = trust killer)
2. Add a favicon (no professional site launches without one)
3. Fix hero `<br>` hardcoded line breaks (broken on mobile)
4. Change "Operators" to "Team" in nav (immediate clarity)

**Top P1s — strong consensus:**
1. Reduce CTA repetition from 9 to 3 max
2. Describe what happens in the Diagnostic Call (30 words in the CTA section or FAQ)
3. Replace the CSS circle-dot logo mark with the diamond mark (logo-v1 SVG)
4. Trim services from 8 tiles to 5 or add a framing note that limits scope expectations
5. Add human voice to at least one section — a first-person paragraph from Rachit

**P2:**
- OG image for social sharing
- "Index 01" eyebrow label cleanup
- Structured FAQ schema markup
- Scroll progress or sticky CTA for long-scroll users
