# Rohit Kapoor — Angel Investor Review

**Who I am:** I invest in early-stage B2B service and SaaS businesses in India. I have seen 200+ pitch decks and websites from founders trying to establish credibility from zero. I can tell the difference between a person who has earned something and a person who is performing it.

**Assignment:** Positioning credibility. Does this look like someone worth betting on? Or is it generic?

---

## Verdict: The operator is credible. The brand is not there yet. The numbers need work.

---

### Credibility Signals That Work

**The in-house accountability argument is strong and real.**

"Most consultancies advise from observation. ScaleBuz was built through in-house accountability."

This is the best line on the site and the core of why this founder is differentiated. Eight companies, real accountability, no retainer safety net. That is a genuine moat. It is stated here but it should be stated earlier — in the hero, not buried in the methodology section.

**The fixed-scope model is the right structural choice.**

Agencies fail because incentives are misaligned — retainers get paid whether pipeline moves or not. Fixed-scope tied to defined outcomes is the right answer. The site communicates this clearly and repeatedly. That is a credibility signal for sophistication.

**Rachit's credentials are legitimate and specific.**

MBA, ISB PGP Pro, senior roles at verifiable companies in recognisable verticals. The bio in the team section reads like a person who has done the work, not a resume assembled to sound impressive.

---

### Credibility Signals That Fail

**The $200M+ pipeline influenced stat is the most dangerous thing on this page.**

I look at numbers professionally. The hero says "$200M+ Pipeline influenced." The case studies on the same page show $2M+ revenue contribution from the AgreeYa engagement, with the NaaS case showing 45% lead improvement and the FinTech showing 35% conversion improvement. There is no arithmetic path from the case study evidence to $200M.

"Pipeline influenced" vs "revenue attributed" is a real distinction — pipeline generated over a 15-year career across 8 companies could plausibly reach $200M. But this is not explained. To any analytical investor or B2B buyer, an unexplained order-of-magnitude gap between a headline stat and supporting case study data looks like inflation. It creates doubt about every other number on the page.

**"50+ Global campaigns delivered" is a filler stat.**

What kind of campaigns? What size? "Campaigns delivered" is a vanity metric that says nothing about outcome. It was added to fill a stat card. Remove it or replace it with something specific.

**The business launched in April 2026 with zero clients.**

Again — I can tell from reading. The case studies are from in-house roles. The site does not disclose this explicitly, which means it is implicitly representing these results as evidence of the consultancy's track record when they are in fact evidence of the founder's in-house career. That distinction matters to sophisticated buyers and does not matter to naive ones. The site should own it directly: "These results come from 15 years of in-house leadership, now applied as an independent practice." The methodology section almost gets there. It does not land the punch.

**Two operators, eight service categories.**

The services section lists 8 distinct capability areas: Strategy, Brand, Demand, Authority, Lifecycle, AI, Web, Events. Two operators. Premium pricing. The implication is that both operators personally deliver across all eight areas. That is not credible to anyone who has managed professional services teams. Either the site needs to acknowledge how the work gets done at scale, or the services list needs to be scoped to what two people can actually own at senior level.

**"ScaleBuz" as a brand name is a liability.**

The camel-casing looks unintentional. "Buz" without the second z looks like a domain compromise. For a premium positioning play in B2B SaaS consulting, the brand name should signal precision and seriousness. This one signals neither.

---

### The Investor Lens Summary

**What I believe:** The operator is real. The methodology is sound. The market need is genuine. The differentiation (in-house accountability, fixed-scope) is legitimate.

**What I am not yet sure of:** Whether this person can close the first 3 clients cold, with no existing brand equity, competing against established fractional CMOs and agencies that have social proof and referral networks.

**What would change my view:** One named client testimonial, or a LinkedIn presence with 3–5 posts demonstrating the quality of thinking, would do more for this brand's credibility than any copy change on this website.

---

### One Thing That Must Be Fixed Before Launch

**Remove or justify the $200M+ stat.** It is the one element that could turn a qualified prospect into a skeptic in the first 10 seconds of serious evaluation.
